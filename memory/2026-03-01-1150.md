user: topic change, how do i know the gender of a sun conure ?
assistant: You usually **can’t tell by looking**—sun conures are **sexually monomorphic** (males and females look the same), so “head shape / color / personality” guesses are unreliable.

**Reliable ways to know:**
1) **DNA sexing (best for most owners):**  
   - Done from **freshly plucked feathers** (needs the follicle/root) *or* a **small blood sample**.  
   - You can buy a mail-in DNA kit, or have an avian vet collect/submit it.

2) **Surgical sexing (endoscopy) by an avian vet:**  
   - Very accurate, but more invasive and usually unnecessary unless the bird already needs a procedure.

**Only “certain” clue without testing:** if your bird **lays an egg**, it’s female (though egg-laying can come with health risks).

If you tell me **your conure’s age** and **why you need to know** (breeding, naming, behavior/health concerns), I’ll recommend the simplest route and how to collect the sample safely.

assistant: Feather-based **DNA sexing is OK at basically any age** (even very young chicks) — there isn’t a “must be X months old” rule for the biology.

What *does* matter is **sample quality**, and that’s where age can indirectly matter:

- **Feathers must be freshly plucked**, not naturally shed. The lab needs cells in the **follicle/root**; a dropped feather often won’t have enough usable DNA.
- **Very young birds** sometimes have **tiny pin/contour feathers**, so some labs may prefer **blood** for babies because it’s a more reliable DNA yield.
- Each lab has its own requirements (often **2–6 plucked feathers** of a certain type/size), so follow the kit instructions.
