assistant: They’re **roughly in the same ballpark** (both splits are on the order of **~tens of millions of years in the Late Cretaceous**), so neither pair is “obviously” much closer than the other.

- **Human ↔ pig:** humans (Euarchontoglires) vs pigs (Laurasiatheria) diverge at the **Boreoeutheria split**, commonly placed around **~85–95 million years ago**.  
  Source: Wikipedia notes Euarchontoglires split from Boreoeutheria ~85–95 Ma (i.e., from the Laurasiatheria side) — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euarchontoglires

- **Parrot ↔ chicken:** parrots are **Neoaves**, chickens are **Galloanserae**; those are the two major branches within modern “other birds” (Neognathae), so their common ancestor is also **deep in modern-bird history**. Neoaves’ crown age is cited around **~72 Ma**, meaning the chicken–parrot split is at least that old and likely not dramatically different from the mammal case.  
  Source: Neoaves overview + temporal range — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neoaves

**Bottom line:** a parrot is **about as closely related** to a chicken as a human is to a pig (same “very distant cousin within the same class” vibe). If forced to choose based on these broad dates alone, **parrot–chicken might be slightly closer**, but the uncertainty is large enough that “about equal” is the honest answer.
